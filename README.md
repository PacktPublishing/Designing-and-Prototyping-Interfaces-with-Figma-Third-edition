  <summary><h2>Requirements for this book</summary>
You will need any modern browser to use the web version of Figma, or alternatively, you can install the Figma desktop app on your computer. The book provides a step-by-step guide to designing an application interface, as well as recommendations for self-practice. To get the most out of the book, it is recommended that you follow the hands-on steps in the following chapters and devote some time to practicing your skills in Figma on your own.</br>

 <p>To follow along with this book, you will need the following:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Figma: Web app (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge) or desktop app (Windows or macOS)</li>
  <li>FigJam: Included within Figma or on iPad (standalone app)</li>
  <li>Figma: Mobile app for iOS/Android (optional)</li>
</ul>
    In <i>Chapter 10</i>, <i>Testing and Sharing Your Prototype in Browsers and Real Devices</i>, you will be asked to test your design on devices with smaller screens, and to do so, you will need to download the Figma app (available for iOS and Android) on your smartphone and/or tablet.

  <summary><h2>Get to know the author</h2></summary>

_Fabio Staiano_ is an interface designer, educator, and Figma Community Leader based in Italy. He holds a degree with honors in communication design and has a background in digital product design developed through both academic training and professional practice.</br>
He spent several years working within the Italian creative agency Geko, later becoming a company partner and contributing to the design and development of digital products for established brands, while actively supporting the local creative community through events and initiatives.</br>
In 2016, he began his career in education at The Guru Lab, where he started teaching user interface and web design, combining practical design workflows with a strong focus on tools and methodologies. In 2019, he further expanded his skill set by attending the Apple Developer Academy, specializing in frontend development and working on multiple app projects and reusable boilerplate solutions.</br>
Today, Fabio works as a design consultant on IT and digital product projects, helping teams structure scalable interface systems and efficient workflows. Alongside his consulting activity, he teaches at private and public design academies in Italy and delivers corporate training programs for companies in Italy and abroad. His work centers on interface design, design systems, and prototyping, and sits at the intersection of professional practice, education, and community engagement.
